Architecture Around the World

Architecture combines art, engineering, and culture to shape the spaces we live in. From sleek glass towers to historic brick facades, each structure tells a story about the people and ideas behind it.

Modern & Minimalist Design
Modern architecture emphasizes clean lines, open floor plans, and the honest use of materials such as glass, steel, and concrete. Minimal ornamentation is used, placing focus on light, proportion, and function. Large windows blur the boundary between interior and exterior, creating bright, flexible spaces for living and working.
Historic & Vernacular Architecture
Historic and vernacular styles are shaped by local climate, materials, and traditions. Thick stone walls, carved wooden details, and patterned brickwork are common elements. These structures often age gracefully, developing a rich patina over time and connecting communities to their cultural heritage and collective memory.

Architecture Around the World

Architecture blends creativity and engineering, shaping the skylines of cities and the character of neighborhoods across the globe.

Key Architectural Styles

Each style of architecture reflects its time, place, and culture. Below are examples that contrast modern and historic approaches.

Modern & Minimalist Design
Modern architecture emphasizes simplicity, open plans, and extensive use of glass and steel. Ornamentation is reduced, and the focus shifts to light, proportion, and functional layouts that support contemporary lifestyles.
Historic & Vernacular Architecture
Historic and vernacular buildings are shaped by local materials, climate, and tradition. Stone, brick, and carved wood often create rich textures and details, preserving cultural identity and connecting communities to their shared past.
